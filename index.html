About Me

Chas Nelson is an enthusiastic interdisciplinary bioscientist with a focus on using physics, engineering and computing in large-scale and high-throughput microscopy and bioimaging. He has completed his Masters in Biology and Physics at Durham University and is currently researching for his PhD in Computing Sciences, also at Durham University.

Current Research

Chas' current research at Durham University focusses on the detection, extraction and analysis of 'blobs' in microscopy images. These 'blobs' can be aggregates of fluorescently tagged proteins, DAPI labelled nuclei or tumours within tissue images.

Teaching

At Durham University Chas is demonstrator for an undergraduate module that introduces 'computational thinking'; and teaches programming skills using the Python scripting language.

Roles at Durham University

Chas also holds various roles across the University: he is, amongst other things, a Trustee for Ustinov College GCR, a Junior Fellow of Durham University's Biophysical Sciences Institute and a postgraduate representative within the School for Engineering and Computing Sciences.
